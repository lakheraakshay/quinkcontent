Study on Advertisement Campaign

I’ve taken the example of a famous car rental company Avis. I’ll be explaining the whole scenario in detail.

In 1962, Avis was in search of a new advertising campaign. Since its inception, the car rental company had trailed behind the market leader, Hertz. So the ad agency Doyle Dane Bernbach decided to embrace Avis’ second-place status as a sneaky way to tout the brand’s customer service. “When you’re only No. 2, you try harder,” went the new tagline.

"The “We Try Harder” ads were an instant hit. Within a year, Avis went from losing $3.2 million to earning $1.2 million—the first time it had been profitable in more than a decade. From 1963 to 1966, as Hertz ignored the Avis campaign, the market-share percentage gap between the two brands shrunk from 61–29 to 49–36. Terrified Hertz executives projected that by 1968 Avis might need a new ad campaign—because it would no longer be No. 2."

We‘ve heard that simple things gets stuck in our mind for longer period of time. Same happened with Avis. The tagline “We try harder” was pretty simple and people started giving Avis a chance. Simple Statements becomes memorable. The tagline “We try harder” became so popular that it actually worked for 50 years and then they introduced a new tagline “Who try Harder” dedicating it to there employees and the people who trusted & supported their work. It was so impactful that even in this decade people are giving its example as one of the best advertisement campaign.

Avis was No. 2 car rental company. Instead of faking the scenario they accepted that they are only No. 2 and that is why they care more for their customers and they try harder as compared to the other competitor. The marketing campaign was totally relevant and interesting. Avis was a loss making company, as the market was totally captured by Hertz before Avis launched its advertisement campaign. As the advertisement was linked to the brand that Avis cares more for their customers, they can’t afford to make their customers wait, they try harder than others. The marketing campaign should always be linked to the brand that’s why it worked for Avis. Avis targeted their customers emotionally as well. They made them understood that even companies care for their customers. After the success of this campaign they launched another one, again targeting their customers and employees through which they showed gratitude and thankfulness these people who have supported them in their tough times.

Marketing campaigns are the perfect way to reach your customers, clients, and leads. They can be the game-changing move that secures meetings with customers or prospects. Producing campaigns with a series of touch points will grab someone's attention better than a single advertisement. You need to break through the clutter when promoting yourself, and marketing campaigns with incentives can do that for you. It is a truism of marketing education that marketing can't create a need, but many marketing campaigns are based on creating an awareness of a product and the desirability of owning that product. What is important is that this _awareness creates the need. Some common strategies for creating an awareness of the product and giving it a context that stimulates a desire to own it are:

• Demonstrating scarcity. Apple, for instance, increased the demand for the Apple 5 by cutting off further shipments of the phone for two weeks immediately after announcing the release.

• Developing a "we" bond between consumers and product, often by announcing the product first to a selected audience, and inviting consumers to participate in the development of the product or product launch.

• Interacting with social media, such as responding to consumer comments, whether favorable or unfavorable.

Marketing is important because it allows businesses to maintain long-lasting and ever-present relationships with their audience. It is not a one-time fix, it is an ongoing strategy that helps businesses flourish. We have seen a lot of cases where an advertisement campaign has turn around the destiny of the company.
